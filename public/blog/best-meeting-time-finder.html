Best Meeting Time Finder: Find Perfect Overlap Across Multiple Timezones

Meeting Tools 10 min read November 4, 2025

Stop manually checking if 3 PM works for everyone. Automatically discover meeting times where all participants are within their working hours.

The Timezone Overlap Problem

With team members in San Francisco, London, and Singapore, finding a time when everyone is awake (let alone at work) is nearly impossible. The timezone spread can be 16+ hours!

Have you experienced this?

  • Spending 30 minutes in a group chat figuring out "what time works for everyone"
  • Accidentally scheduling calls at 3 AM for someone
  • The frustration of "I can't do that time, I'll be asleep"
  • Endless back-and-forth trying to find a compromise

How Best Meeting Time Finder Works

  1. Add all participant timezones to GeoSyncX
  2. The tool analyzes working hours (9 AM - 6 PM) for each timezone
  3. It finds overlapping windows where everyone is available
  4. Results are color-coded: green (all available), yellow (some outside hours), red (bad time)

The tool shows you the best compromise times based on your priorities.

Pro Tip: The "Golden Window"

For most US-Europe-Asia combinations, 6-8 AM Pacific / 2-4 PM London / 9-11 PM Singapore is often the only viable overlap. The tool identifies these windows automatically.

When Perfect Overlap Doesn't Exist

Sometimes there's no perfect time - and that's okay. The tool helps you find the "least bad" option by showing:

  • Exactly who would be inconvenienced
  • By how much (e.g., "30 minutes before working hours" vs "2 AM")
  • Alternative days where overlap might be better

Common Scenarios

US + Europe

Usually easy - late US morning (10 AM - 12 PM ET) overlaps with European afternoon (3-5 PM GMT).

US + India

Challenging - US morning means India evening/night. Best overlap is early US morning (8-9 AM ET = 5:30-6:30 PM IST).

US + East Asia (Japan, Singapore, Australia)

Most difficult - very early US morning or very late US evening required. Consider async alternatives.
