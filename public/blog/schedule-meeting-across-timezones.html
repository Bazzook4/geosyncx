How to Schedule Meetings Across Timezones: Google Calendar Integration

Published: November 4, 2025 · 9 min read · Category: Meeting Tools

Stop manually creating calendar invites with timezone notes. Automatically schedule meetings that show correct local time for every attendee.

The International Meeting Challenge

Scheduling a meeting with people in New York, London, and Tokyo? You need to:

  • Calculate the right time for each timezone
  • Create calendar invites with correct times
  • Add notes explaining "This is 3 PM your time"
  • Hope nobody gets confused

The GeoSyncX Solution

GeoSyncX integrates with Google Calendar to create meeting invites that automatically show the correct local time for each attendee.

How to Schedule a Meeting

  1. Add all participant timezones to your comparison list
  2. Set your primary timezone
  3. Choose a date and time
  4. See the converted time for each timezone
  5. Click "Add to Google Calendar"
  6. The event is created with all timezone information

What Gets Created

  • Calendar event at the correct UTC time
  • Description showing local times for all participants
  • Each recipient sees the meeting in their local time

Best Practices

  • Always double-check the converted times before sending
  • Consider working hours - avoid scheduling at 3 AM for anyone
  • Use the Best Meeting Time Finder to find optimal overlap
